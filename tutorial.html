ZEPHYRAEN

Calibration Briefing

Backstory

On March 14th, 2047, an asteroid struck Siberia. It carried a virus — the Zephyr Strain — and within days, every insect on Earth had mutated. Larger. Faster. Lethal. The swarms came quickly, and humanity was not ready. Ninety-five percent of the population was gone before anyone understood what was happening.

The survivors fought back the only way they could. Scientists developed the CHITIN-FRAME — an exosuit that fused with its host, creating a human-AI hybrid soldier called an AEGIS. You were selected, suited up, and placed in a facility for calibration. You never got the chance to finish. The facility was attacked, the scientists were killed, and a dying researcher pressed a data chip into your hand with her last breath. Four words: Find the crater. Survive.

You crawled out of the rubble alone. The suit is running at twelve percent. The chip is locked. The world outside is unrecognisable. You don't know what's on the chip, you don't know what's at the crater, and you don't know why she sent you there. You just know you're the only AEGIS left — and that somewhere out in this broken world, the answers are waiting.

04 // METAMORPHOSIS [LEVEL 20]

CALIBRATION THRESHOLD: 100%
TRIGGER: AUTOMATIC AT LEVEL 20.
PROCESS: IRREVERSIBLE.
// The suit does not ask. It decides. Then it offers you a choice.

At Level 20, the CHITIN-FRAME reaches full calibration. By then, it has been studying your choices, reflexes, and stat priorities for a long time. At that point it triggers Metamorphosis, permanently reshaping the suit around your dominant style.

You will be offered two class options based on how you built your character. You can choose one. The change is immediate, permanent, and it will define how your suit performs from then on.

AVAILABLE CLASSES:
PHANTOM — AGI / PER build. Speed, evasion, precision.
RAVAGER — STR / END build. Power, aggression, endurance.
BULWARK — VIT / END build. Defence, resilience, protection.
SOVEREIGN — Balanced build. Adaptive, versatile, command.

COMBAT

CHITIN-FRAME GUIDE // PAGE 04

CHITIN-FRAME COMBAT SYSTEMS: ONLINE.

// Think before you act. You have time. Use it.

Combat is turn-based. No reaction tests, no twitch timing. You get time to think, and each choice matters. Most bad fights are lost because of one rushed decision.

YOUR ACTIONS

ACTION COST WHAT IT DOES
ATTACK Free Standard strike. Damage scales with STR and equipped weapon.
SKILL Stamina A CHITIN-FRAME ability. Usually stronger than a basic attack.
ITEM Free Use a consumable. Does not cost Stamina.
ANALYSE One turn Study the enemy. Reveals weaknesses and key mechanics.
RETREAT One turn Attempt to flee. Success depends on your AGI.

DAMAGE & DEFENCE

Your damage comes from STR plus your weapon. Enemy defence lowers what gets through. AGI helps you avoid hits, and VIT helps you survive the ones that land. Check the CHITIN-FRAME readout before committing.

THREAT LEVELS

LOW The fight is in your favour. Engage.
MODERATE Near your level. Have a plan.
HIGH Outscales your current build. Prepare carefully or avoid.
EXTREME It will kill you. Leave. Come back later.

EXTREME is not a challenge rating. It is a statement of fact.

STATUS EFFECTS

Mutant insects can apply debuffs, and those effects can stack fast. That is where many fights go wrong. Always ANALYSE enemies you have not seen before. One turn spent learning can save the whole run.

  • CORRODE — acid damage that degrades your defence over time
  • BLIND — spore-based, dramatically reduces your accuracy
  • VENOM — ongoing HP loss per turn until treated
  • STAGGER — heavy impact, causes you to lose your next turn

HAVEN is built by you, from nothing. There is no ready-made town waiting for your leadership. Every wall, structure, and survivor outcome comes from your decisions. This is not a side system. It is your foundation.

SURVIVORS & MORALE

Every survivor has skills and morale. Morale is not cosmetic. High morale gives useful bonuses, low morale creates real problems. The state of HAVEN reflects how you manage the people in it.

  • High morale — bonus resource output, unique NPC dialogue
  • Mid morale — normal function, no penalty
  • Low morale — hoarding, arguments, risk of desertion

Everything you collect goes into the CHITIN-FRAME inventory. Carry limit scales with STR and END. If you go over it, the suit slows down. Plan your load before long expeditions.

CRAFTING

Crafting happens at HAVEN, not in the field. The Workshop handles weapons and suit repairs. The Infirmary handles medicine. Prepare before you leave, because running out of medkits far from home usually ends badly.

THE CODEX & CLUE SYSTEM

CHITIN-FRAME GUIDE // PAGE 07

CODEX STATUS: ACTIVE.

ENTRIES LOGGED: 0.

// Something happened to this world. The answer is out there. In pieces.

The Codex is the CHITIN-FRAME record of what you have found and what you think it means. It does not hand you full answers. It gives pieces. Some players will finish Part I with a clear picture. Others will still be piecing things together.

CLUE FRAGMENTS

Clue Fragments can drop after combat. INT and PER improve your chances. Bosses always drop one, and boss clues usually matter. Take hard fights if you want real answers.

Common enemies Small chance per kill. Improved by INT and PER stats.
Mid-tier enemies Slightly better odds. Worth pursuing.
Boss kills Guaranteed drop. Always a major Codex entry.

CODEX ENTRY TYPES

  • Biological — insect mutation data, behaviour anomalies, physical observations
  • Historical — pre-incident records, government data, research notes
  • Signal — audio and frequency data captured by the suit's passive scanner
  • Unknown — entries the CHITIN-FRAME cannot classify. Pay attention to these.

BREAKTHROUGHS

When the CHITIN-FRAME links related Fragments, it creates a Breakthrough. That means a deeper insight that no single clue could give on its own. Breakthroughs grant bonus EXP and unlock new Codex sections. Some only trigger when clues are found in a specific order.

// You can also cross-reference entries manually. The suit hasn't connected everything yet.

THE DATA CHIP

The chip Dr. Vasquez gave you is the most important item in the Codex. It stays locked until the CHITIN-FRAME reaches enough calibration. When it opens, it changes how you understand everything that came before it.
